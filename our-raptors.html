Our Philosophy

A Bond Built on Trust & Respect

At Falconry in Ireland, every bird in our care is treated as a wild soul first and a partner second. Our falconers dedicate years to understanding raptor behaviour, building relationships rooted in patience, mutual trust, and deep respect for these extraordinary creatures.

Our birds are not merely trained performers — they are ambassadors of Ireland's living falconry heritage, each with their own personality, history, and story to tell. When you meet them, you step into a tradition stretching back over 4,000 years.

Featured Species

Falco peregrinus

The Peregrine Falcon

The fastest creature on Earth — the Peregrine Falcon is the crown jewel of falconry. Reaching speeds of over 380 km/h in a hunting stoop, this bird has been prized by falconers across cultures and centuries, from medieval Irish chieftains to modern-day enthusiasts.

Ireland's wild coasts and cliffs are home to nesting peregrine populations, and our resident birds carry that same wild spirit. Training a peregrine is a lifelong commitment and one of falconry's most prestigious pursuits.

  • Top Speed: Up to 389 km/h in a hunting stoop
  • Wingspan: 89–120 cm
  • Diet: Medium-sized birds, primarily caught in flight
  • Status: Conservation success story in Ireland

Our Residents

Meet the Full Mews

From the stoic Harris Hawk to the silent barn owl, each bird in our care brings its own chapter to Ireland's falconry story.

Parabuteo unicinctus

Harris Hawk

The sociable hunter of the raptor world. Harris Hawks are unique for hunting in cooperative family groups — a trait that makes them exceptionally responsive to falconers and ideal for beginners and experienced handlers alike.

Beginner Friendly Social Hunter

Buteo jamaicensis

Red-Tailed Hawk

One of the most iconic raptors in the falconry world. The Red-tailed Hawk is known for its calm temperament, impressive wingspan, and the distinctive brick-red tail that gives it its name. A powerful and reliable hunting partner.

Calm Temperament Powerful Hunter

Tyto alba

Barn Owl

Ireland's ghostly night hunter. The Barn Owl is deeply woven into Irish folklore and countryside tradition. With silent flight and extraordinary hearing, it navigates total darkness with ease — a living piece of the Irish landscape's magic.

Nocturnal Irish Folklore

Bubo bubo

Eurasian Eagle-Owl

Europe's largest owl and one of falconry's most awe-inspiring presences. The Eurasian Eagle-Owl's piercing orange eyes and imposing wingspan of nearly two metres leave a lasting impression on every visitor fortunate enough to meet one.

Europe's Largest Owl Majestic

Falco columbarius

Merlin

Ireland's smallest falcon and a bird steeped in Celtic myth. The Merlin was historically the 'lady's hawk' in medieval falconry — a spirited, agile flyer that punches well above its weight. Named, some say, after the legendary wizard himself.

Celtic Heritage Agile Flyer

Falco tinnunculus

Common Kestrel

Ireland's most recognisable roadside raptor. The Kestrel is famous for its trademark hover — hanging perfectly still in the wind while scanning the ground below. A true Irish native and beloved countryside companion throughout the seasons.

Irish Native Expert Hoverer

Welfare First

Care & Training Philosophy

Responsible falconry means putting the bird's wellbeing above all else. Our approach blends traditional methods with modern avian science.

Natural Diet

Species-appropriate, high-quality fresh food sourced ethically to reflect each bird's natural prey items and nutritional requirements.

Spacious Mews

Custom-built weathering and housing facilities designed to minimise stress and maximise comfort for each individual bird species.

Positive Training

All training is based on positive reinforcement. We never force a bird to fly — every interaction is voluntary and built on genuine trust.

Veterinary Care

Regular health checks with specialist avian veterinarians ensure every bird is in peak physical condition throughout the year.
