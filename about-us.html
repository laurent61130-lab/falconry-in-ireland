The sky over Ireland has always been a living canvas. For centuries, the flight of a hawk across a green valley was not just a spectacle — it was a conversation between the earth and the heavens.

How It Began

Thirty Years of Falconry in Ireland

That ancient dialogue captured my heart when I was a boy, standing on a wind‑whipped hillside in County Mayo, watching a peregrine fold its wings and drop like a prayer. I never truly looked away.

My name is Liam O'Connor. I have spent more than thirty years walking the bogs, cliffs, and woodlands of this island with a bird on my glove. My journey began in the traditional way: an apprenticeship of silence, patience, and deep listening. I learned from men and women who carried knowledge that no book could contain — knowledge passed down through generations, whispered into the feathers of a young goshawk or hummed to a sleepy long‑eared owl at dawn.

Falconry is one of humanity's oldest relationships with the wild. Inscribed on the UNESCO Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity, it is not a sport but a living heritage. In Ireland, that heritage feels especially close to the bone — when you fly a hawk here, you slip into a story that began before the first stone was laid at Newgrange.

The Team

Who We Are

Falconry Experiences in Ireland was born from a simple desire: to share this extraordinary bond with people who crave something real. We are a small, family‑run team of falconers, conservationists, and storytellers. Every member of our team has dedicated their life to birds of prey — not as exhibits but as partners.

We live with them. We hunt with them. We celebrate their wildness and protect their welfare with fierce devotion. Our birds are not trained to perform; they are invited to reveal their nature. The trust between falconer and hawk is a delicate, honest thing, and we guard it carefully.

Come and meet our birds — each one has a name, a personality, and a story of their own.

Where We Fly

Falconry Experiences in the West of Ireland

We have chosen the West of Ireland as our home because this is a place where the light shifts every moment, where the Atlantic wind keeps the air clean, and where you can still find silence deep enough to hear the beat of a raven's wing.

Every falconry experience we offer is designed to immerse you in that environment — not as a spectator, but as a participant in something ancient. Whether you are taking your first trembling step with a Harris hawk in one of our beginner sessions, or spending a half‑day wandering through ruined abbeys with a golden eagle on our heritage walks, we promise you an encounter that is gentle, respectful, and unforgettable.

Living Tradition

We honour falconry as a UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage, practising and teaching it with the same reverence it has commanded for over 4,000 years.

Raptor Welfare

The wellbeing of our birds is paramount. Every raptor in our care lives in species-appropriate conditions and is handled only by trained professionals following strict ethical standards.

Conservation & Education

We are active in raptor conservation, habitat restoration, and education. Once a person has felt the weight of a hawk on their fist, they become a guardian of the wild places.

UNESCO Recognised

A Living Art, Recognised by the World

Above all, we believe in hospitality that has roots. When you arrive at our mews, you become part of our extended family. There will be strong tea, warm words, and no hurry. The birds will tell us when it is time to fly. Everything we do is guided by the rhythm of the creatures we love, and that rhythm is one of the oldest in the world.
